Nanophotonic Design in Organic Photovoltaics Devices

External Radiative Efficiency Simulation for Organic Photovoltaics    The primary research topic focuses on accurately quantifying the non-radiation losses, particularly by evaluating the external radiative efficiency (ERE) of photovoltaic devices based on Planck’s Law and Schockley-Queisser limit. Through theorical modeling and simulations, this work investigates the origins of efficiency losses and examines the relationship between solar cell performance and calculated ERE value.

Nanophotonic Design

Nanophotonic Design Enhancing ERE    Photonic design in photovoltaics, which involves introducing specific nanopatterns, enables efficient light-trapping and light-coupling in semiconductor mediums of organic photovoltaics, controls radiative recombination of excitons, thereby enhancing solar cell efficiency. This approach could help push solar cells closer to their theoretical efficiency limit. Beyond photovoltaics, photonic design plays a pivotal role in the semiconductor industry, with applications extending to silicon photonics integrated circuit which enables high-capacity optical communications.

Optical Research

Optical Characterization    Steady-state optical properties were investigated using photoluminescence (PL) spectroscopy and PL quantum yield (PLQY) measurements to evaluate the macroscale radiative efficiency enhancement. Time-resolved measurements were performed using two complementary techniques: ultrafast spectroscopy (PicoQuant system) for lifetime measurements and femtosecond-resolved PL spectroscopy (collaboration with Prof. Piotrowiak's group at Rutgers-Newark) to analyze exciton dynamics at the picosecond timescale, particularly focusing on triplet state transfer processes.

Device Fabrication

Ring Resonator    Devices were fabricated using physical vapor deposition (PVD) and sputtering techniques. Both conventional and inverted device architectures were successfully fabricated, described in Preliminary data part below. Solar cell performance was characterized using a solar simulator (Rutgers Cleanroom facility) under standard AM 1.5G illumination conditions. Future work will incorporate photonic design elements into the device architecture, with plans to measure external quantum efficiency (EQE) of device through potential external collaborations.
